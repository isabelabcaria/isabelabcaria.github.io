Insurance of the future

When uncertainty strikes reality, radical shifts are caused in people’s needs, behaviours and expectations. It requires quick adaptation. How can the insurance industry become more customer centered and innovative in 2030? A communal insurance that goes beyond home and content, can help these organizations act more into prevention than intervention.

Role

Research
Concept development
Business strategy

Output

A communal insurance that goes beyond home and content reinforcing trust in the community to reach environmental goals together.

Collaborators

Corina Vladut, Kristelle Achkar, Carla Camerlingo, Vitor Gobatto and Jeroen Ernst​

Context

XXXX

The project was developed with and for a global insurance organization that aims to row towards a human centric approach while acting more into prevention of risks than only acting in those single moments when the risk becomes reality to their customers.

Challenge

In order to help them pursue this path, the challenge was to present two future scenarios (a possible and a preposterous one) for their persona in 2030 regarding personal life and community, financial and family care, health and wellbeing, comsumption, mobility and travel, and work; find opportunity areas that connected with their strategy and develop a general strategic advice.

Process

A system map was built during the research phase to analyse the systems associated to the needs of the persona presented by the company and map the risks that could impact those needs aiming to find business opportunities.

We dig deep into 2030 trends around work and knowledge, automation, consumption, financial and family care, health and wellbeing, mobility and travel, future of insurance and how Generations Y and Z would relate to them. On the interviews we focused on understanding current needs, behaviors, motivations and routines, as well as future thoughts of people in their 20s and 30s regarding the same topics.

Findings

Among other discoveries, we found out that both people in their 20s and in their 30s believe in an increasing prevalence of communal living in the future, that there's lack of knowledge about data usage and an unsafe feeling about it. Specially Generation Z see that insurance should help with global causes (social, environmental), for example, unemployment, people that do not have access to their services, people on the streets, nature. These findings guided our design process.

Building future scenarios

By comparing and projecting the younger generation into the older one, we were able to define future personas.

Synthetizing and connecting trends and insights from the interviews allowed us to develop 2 future scenarios. In the possible scenario, Data Controls Your Life, life is completely remote and there is transparency of data. In the preposterous scenario, Sustainability Über Alles, sustainability plays a very strong role and there is no transparency of data.

Narratives were created for each scenario considering multiple spheres of this society that that this future persona would be living in 2030 encompassing economic, governmental, political, social and cultural aspects. This helped us ground the scenario in order to spot the opportunities and generate ideas that connected to the objectives of the company.

Scenario: Sustainability über alles
Scenario: Data controls your life

One of the opportunities spotted in this scenario was regarding shared insurance between a community. In order to understand behaviors and thoughts around this concept in a future like the one described in this scenario, trigger cards were built and tested with the peoples in their 20s. A toolkit was built to share with the client so they could use within the company.

Solution

Communal insurance

A communal insurance that goes beyond home and content reinforcing trust in the community to reach environmental goals together.

Through this service, the insurance company enables, strengthens and facilitates the integration of the community members through the instructions/tasks given in the Shared Insurance Platform. Since the tasks can be shared between the people who live there, they are able to go through experiences to secure the community and reach their individual and common environmental goals (such as targets of carbon emissions or water footprint). By being inside the community helping people protect their homes, their love ones and accomplish environmental goals, the company itself gets greener and accomplishes its own duties with nature, thus preventing harm not only to the community but also to their very first common home: the planet.

Strategic advice

If the company had to start acting tomorrow to take some steps towards the future scenario and service presented, what would be advised to be done?

Besides providing a Blueprint of the service and building the Business Model and Value Proposition Canvas, the team proposed a strategic advice in order for the client to develop the service, the sustainability über alles scenario happening or not.
