Insurance of the future

Tourism is a strategic sector and one of the economic driving forces of the Catalan economy. How can tourists easily discover unique places tourism providers attract more tourists and avoid seasonality. Grand Adventure Catalunya is a scalable solution that inspires change in a post pandemic world.

Role

Research, concept development and business strategy

Output

A communal insurance that goes beyond home and content reinforcing trust in the community to reach environmental goals together.

Collaborators

Corina Vladut, Kristelle Achkar, Carla Camerlingo, Vitor Gobatto and Jeroen Ernst​

Context

XXXX

The project was developed with and for a global insurance organization that aims to row towards a human centric approach while acting more into prevention of risks than only acting in those single moments when the risk becomes reality to their customers.

Challenge

In order to help them pursue this path, the challenge was to present two future scenarios (one more probable and one more disruptive) for their persona in 2030 regarding personal life and community, financial and family care, health and wellbeing, comsumption, mobility and travel, and work; find opportunity areas that connected with their strategy and develop a general strategic advice.

Process

A system map was built during the research phase to analyse the systems associated to the needs of the persona presented by the company and map the risks that could impact those needs aiming to find business opportunities.

We dig deep into 2030 trends around work and knowledge, automation, consumption, financial and family care, health and wellbeing, mobility and travel, future of insurance and how Generations Y and Z would relate to them. On the interviews we focused on understanding current needs, behaviors, motivations and routines, as well as future thoughts of people in their 20s and 30s regarding the same topics.

Findings

Among other discoveries, we found out that both people in their 20s and in their 30s believe in an increasing prevalence of communal living in the future, that there's lack of knowledge about data usage and an unsafe feeling about it. Specially Generation Z see that insurance should help with global causes (social, environmental), for example, unemployment, people that do not have access to their services, people on the streets, nature. This finding guided our design process.

Building future scenarios

By comparing and projecting the younger generation into the older one, we were able to define future personas.

Synthetizing and connecting trends and insights from the interviews allowed us to develop 2 future scenarios. In the possible scenario, Data Controls Your Life, life is completely remote and there is transparency of data. In the preposterous scenario, Sustainability Über Alles, sustainability plays a very strong role and there is no transparency of data.

Narratives were created for each scenario considering multiple spheres of this society that that this future persona would be living in 2030 encompassing economic, governmental, political, social and cultural aspects. This helped us ground the scenario in order to spot the opportunities and generate ideas that connected to the objectives of the company.

Concept

​​The concept was based on the idea of variable reward from product psychology. It involves moving from a conventional feedback loop to a new habit-forming mechanism to hook users: the Hook Model.

Variable rewards apparently keep the brain busy, clearing away its defenses and opening space for generating new habits. The idea is based on the reward of the self, of the tribe and of the hunt.

Solution

Web app for tourists

The idea was inspired by the concept of yield management mostly used by airlines. Typically the earlier the customer makes the reservation, lower fares might be available, but if they decide to make a last minute booking, they will probably pay the full fare.

Based on it, tourist flow is organized across the regions and tourism providers have access to the information.

Impact

  • • We made the concept operational and designed the strategy through the Blueprint and the Business Model Canvas. This grounded the ideas to the client.
  • • We delivered a gamified experience to the client that went beyond their expectations besides being a scalable solution.
  • • The Yield Management concept inspired in the CTB with future directions for the tourism sector.
  • • We generated meaningful discussions around the changes in tourism specially in a post pandemic world.
  • The project is still being developed and might be implemented within the next one or two years.
